# Sources and References: Baku (獏)

## Primary Historical Sources

### Ancient Chinese Texts

1. *Erya* (爾雅, Approaching Elegance). Compiled c. 3rd century BCE. Earliest Chinese dictionary mentioning *mo* (貘). Various editions.

2. *Shen Yi Jing* (神異経, Classic of Divine Marvels). Attributed to Dongfang Shuo (東方朔), c. 220 CE. Describes supernatural beasts including *mo*. Translated sections in various comparative mythology works.

3. Li Shizhen (李時珍). *Bencao Gangmu* (本草綱目, Compendium of Materia Medica). 1578. Includes entry on *mo* with medicinal and protective properties. English translation by Luo Xiwen. Foreign Languages Press, 2003.

4. *Shan Hai Jing* (山海経, Classic of Mountains and Seas). Compiled 4th-1st century BCE. Various mythical beasts providing context for *mo* tradition. Translation by Anne Birrell. Penguin Classics, 1999.

### Japanese Historical Texts

5. Terajima Ryōan (寺島良安). *Wakan Sansai Zue* (和漢三才図会, Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia). 1712. 105 volumes. Comprehensive entry on Baku. National Diet Library Digital Collection.

6. Toriyama Sekien (鳥山石燕). *Gazu Hyakki Yagyō* (画図百鬼夜行). 1776. Illustrated Baku among supernatural creatures. Facsimile edition by Kadokawa Shoten, 1999.

7. Toriyama Sekien. *Konjaku Gazu Zoku Hyakki* (今昔画図続百鬼). 1779. Additional Baku imagery and description. Critical edition by Komatsu Kazuhiko, 2005.

8. Takehara Shunsen (竹原春泉). *Ehon Hyaku Monogatari* (絵本百物語). 1841. Picture book including Baku. Kokusho Kankōkai facsimile, 1988.

9. Sawaki Sūshi (佐脇嵩之). *Hyakkai Zukan* (百怪図巻). 1737. Illustrated demon scroll with Baku. National Diet Library Digital Collection.

### Edo Period Literature

10. Various *kaidan* (怪談, ghost story) collections. Edo period. References to Baku in nightmare contexts. Multiple publishers and authors.

11. *Kibyōshi* (黄表紙, yellow-cover books). Late 18th century. Popular illustrated fiction including Baku references.

12. Santō Kyōden (山東京伝). Various works including supernatural themes and Baku mentions. Late 18th-early 19th century.

13. Matsura Seizan (松浦静山). *Kasshi Yawa* (甲子夜話). 1821-1841. Essays and anecdotes including folklore references.
